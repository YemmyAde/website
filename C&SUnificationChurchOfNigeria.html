Cherubim & Seraphim Unification Church of Nigeria

Cherubim & Seraphim Unification Church of Nigeria was registered on 9th September 1999. It is a Church of Churches as its membership is composed mainly of Churches of the Cherubim & Seraphim sect. The Cherubim & Seraphim Church itself came into existence on 9th September 1925 when it was first named “Seraphim” to which “Cherubim” was later added in the same year.

It's antecedent was however laid in 1879 with the birth of Moses Orimolade and his extensive evangelical work and "prayer" prowess backed by divine power of God In June 1925 Moses Orimolade had a mysterious encounter with a young lady named Christiana Abiodun Akinsowon. The encounter led to the emergence of a prayer/revival fellowship in which the duo of Orimolade and Abiodun played prominent roles. In 1929 an internal squabble led to the division of the C&S into two groups with Orimolade leading one and Abiodun the other.

Further divisions followed which created different factions. This proliferation of the C&S Churches brought with it disunity and adulteration of the liturgy and practices of the sect from what they originally were; with different brand names Thus the primary aim of Cherubim & Seraphim Unification Church of Nigeria or “the Unification” is to restore the unity of the C&S Churches and as much as possible standardize the liturgy and practices of the sect.

All the activities of the Church centre around the bringing together of all C&S churches to speak and do things in one accord under one leadership and promote unity For ease of operation, the C&S Unification Church has Chapters including the Abuja Chapter of which the Chairman of C&S Movement Church Dakibiyu District, Special Apostle Pastor (Dr.) A. A. Bodunrin is the current Grand Patron.
